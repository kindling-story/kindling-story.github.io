An Unordered Collection

| “It is not one voice, but a chorus arguing with itself. Each page insists its own truth, even as the next undoes it”

A large set of many volumes varying in size and construction, and a plethora of smaller individual papers. Some are wrapped in cloth, others in pressed bark. The papers both separate and bound vary in thickness and texture. Many are water-swollen, ink bleeding.
Discovered in a house apart from the village. Stacked floor to ceiling, on shelves and slopes and any surface with strength to carry. Boxes overflowing and piles collapsing, there are is more than a lifetime’s paper to contend with.
Conditions vary wildly, affected by their location within the house and the care put into their construction.

Contents
Yet to catalogue

This collection speaks with authority but not certainty. There is truth in their scattered pages, obscured by many contexts, but much speculation too. I find comfort in their confusion. Their errors are warm, human. Their voices, though long extinguished, still argue in the dark.
